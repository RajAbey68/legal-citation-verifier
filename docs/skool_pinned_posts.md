## POST 2 — 📐 Writing Standards (Voice, Style, Kill List)

**Title:** 📐 Writing Standards — Voice, Style & Kill List

---

Every chapter must pass three tests before it reaches APPROVED status:

1. **Flesch Reading Ease ≥ 75** (Ch09 minimum: 65)
2. **Zero Kill List violations**
3. **Author voice preserved**

---

### Author Voice Profiles

**Rajiv Abeysinghe (Ch 1, 3, 4, 5, 10)**
- Structured-conversational, concept-led, contrarian framing
- Pattern: Context → reframe → angle → next step
- Openings: Specific timestamp (8:47am, not "early morning"). Concrete location. Problem immediately visible.
- Sentences: 12–15 words average, max 20. Active voice throughout.
- Banned: Passive voice where active works | "It is worth noting" | "In order to" | "This demonstrates"

**Darren Sylvester (Ch 2, 6, 11, 12)**
- "You" voice ALWAYS. Evidence-first, scenario-led.
- Pattern: Problem → data → solution → action
- Openings: Direct address ("You've identified..."). No third-person observation.
- Sentences: 10–12 words average. Short paragraphs (2–4 sentences).
- Banned: Third-person ("The firm discovered...") | Passive voice | "Perhaps consider..." | "You could potentially..."

**Nick Lockett (Ch 7, 8, 9)**
- Triadic structure, parenthetical precision, direct attribution.
- Pattern: Framework → three variants → implications. ALWAYS IN THREES.
- Openings: Specific date + time. Short declarative facts. No scene-setting warmup.
- Attribution: [Authority] requires/prohibits/mandates [specific requirement].
- Sentences: 14–16 words average.
- Banned: "Might suggest" | "Could indicate" | "Generally speaking" | Emotional language

**Sushila Nair (security sections in Ch 4, 8)**
- Standards-precise, audit-ready language
- ISO/ISACA terminology used correctly with full expansion on first use
- Every control mapped to a specific standard clause

---

### Mandatory Chapter Structure

Every chapter must contain these sections in order:

1. RELEVANCE FILTER (TLDR) — who should read, who can skip
2. THE SCENARIO — opening scene (400–600 words)
3. THE LOGIC — 3 points explaining why
4. THE BLUEPRINT — 7–10 step implementation
5. THE ROI — time investment + outcomes
6. DELIVERABLES — 5–7 concrete tools
7. CITATIONS — Tier 1 sources only
8. RISKS — 4–6 risks with mitigations
9. PRACTITIONER DISCLAIMER

---

### The Kill List — Zero Tolerance

Delete or rewrite any of the following on sight:

**AI smell words:**
Transformative | Delve | Delving | Landscape (business context) | Game-changer | Game-changing | Seamless | Seamlessly | Leverage (as verb — use "use") | Navigate (business context) | Robust (be specific) | Synergy | Paradigm | Holistic | Cutting-edge | Revolutionary | Innovative (show don't tell) | Disruptive | Ecosystem | Low-hanging fruit

**Banned phrases:**
"It is worth noting that..." | "In order to..." (→ use "To...") | "Due to the fact that..." (→ use "Because...") | "In today's rapidly evolving world..." | "In conclusion..." | "This highlights the importance of..." | "X is not just Y but also Z..." | "This demonstrates..." | "It could be argued that..." | "Studies show..." (without naming the study) | "Research indicates..." (without citation) | "Perhaps consider..." | "You could potentially..." | "Generally speaking" | "Straightforward"

**Style rules:**
- No passive voice where active works
- No generic description where specific detail is available
- No hedging without named evidence

---

### Flesch Targets

| Benchmark | Flesch |
|-----------|--------|
| SRA enforcement notices | 80–85 |
| Which? magazine | ~80 |
| **Our target** | **75–85** |
| Ch09 hard ceiling (EU AI Act) | 65+ |

Five principles to hit the target:
1. **Punch opener** — every section starts in 6–10 words
2. **Rhythm rule** — medium (12–16w) → medium → SHORT (≤6w)
3. **Complexity into structure** — comparisons and lists → bullets/tables
4. **Earned conviction** — reader arrives at urgency through evidence, not advocacy
5. **Consequence of not acting** — every major section names what happens if they do nothing

## POST 3 — 🔍 Evidence Hierarchy & Citation Protocol

**Title:** 🔍 Evidence Hierarchy & Citation Protocol

---

Every claim in this book must be traceable to a source. No exceptions.

---

### The Three Tiers

**TIER 1 — Cite directly**
These sources are authoritative. Quote or paraphrase with full attribution.

- SRA (sra.org.uk)
- Law Society (lawsociety.org.uk)
- EUR-Lex (eur-lex.europa.eu)
- UK legislation (legislation.gov.uk)
- Thomson Reuters legal reports (named report + date)
- Legal Futures (named journalist + date)
- Law Society Gazette (named journalist + date)
- Clio UK legal industry reports (named, dated)

**TIER 2 — Label as practitioner-developed**
These are our frameworks. They are valuable but NOT official standards.

Task Classification Matrix | Shadow Efficiency Calculator | HITL Register | Story Files | Median Costing Method | Margin Liberation Model | Technology Register | Governance Ownership Matrix | SRA Show File | EU AI Act Compliance Dashboard | PI Insurance Evidence Package | Exit Interview Protocol

Every Tier 2 reference must be accompanied by the Practitioner Disclaimer.

**TIER 3 — Label as illustrative**
Fictional characters and composite scenarios. Always clearly flagged.

Sarah Mitchell | Emily Patterson | David Harrison | Jordan Hayes | James Stewart | Sophie Williams | Marcus Thompson | Robert Ashford | Claire

**DELETE if none apply:**
"approximately X%" | "around X%" | "industry average" | "studies show" without a named Tier 1 source.

---

### Copyright Rules — CRITICAL

- **Maximum 15 words** per direct quote from any source
- **Maximum ONE quote** per source per chapter — after quoting once, that source is closed. Paraphrase everything else.
- Never reproduce song lyrics, poems, or haikus
- After quoting a Tier 1 source once, use paraphrase + citation for all subsequent references

---

### The Three RISK 5 Fabrications — Never Repeat

These three references appeared in early drafts. They do not exist. They must never appear in any chapter.

| ❌ Fabricated | ✅ Correct replacement |
|---|---|
| "GDPR Article 9 + SRA Outcome 6.3 require UK data residency" | "UK GDPR Chapter V permits international transfers via adequacy decisions, SCCs, or BCRs. UK data residency is preferred for simplicity but not legally mandatory." |
| "SRA Technology Guidance 2024" | "SRA Standards & Regulations (current as of 2025)" or specific Code Outcome numbers (e.g. Code Outcome 8.5) |
| "Law Society PI Insurance Practice Note 2025" | "Insurance Act 2015 Section 3 (duty of fair presentation) and Section 8 (warranties)" |

The integrity check script (`integrity_check.ts`) will flag these as CRITICAL on every run.

---

### Citation Format

In-text: (Source Name, Year) or direct attribution — "The SRA's Standards & Regulations require..."
End of chapter: Full reference list, Tier 1 sources only, in this format:
> [Author/Organisation]. *[Title]*. [Publisher/URL]. [Date accessed or published].

## POST 4 — 📊 Four-Eyes Verification Framework

**Title:** 📊 Four-Eyes Verification Framework

---

No chapter reaches COMPLETE status without passing Four-Eyes verification.

This is not optional. It is the final gate before a chapter is submitted to Law Society Publishing.

---

### What Four-Eyes Checks

Every chapter must be independently verified across four dimensions:

**1. Citation Integrity**
- Every Tier 1 citation exists and says what the text claims
- No fabricated references (RISK 5 list checked)
- No "approximately", "around", or "studies show" without a named source
- Copyright limits respected (15 words max, one quote per source)

**2. Regulatory Accuracy**
- All SRA references checked against current published guidance
- EU AI Act article numbers and enforcement dates verified
- No practitioner frameworks presented as SRA standards
- Practitioner Disclaimer present where required

**3. Character Data Consistency**
All canonical figures are locked. These cannot change between chapters:

| Character | Locked data |
|-----------|-------------|
| Sarah Mitchell | 5,400 trapped hours | 90-day ROI 628% | Year 1 ROI 1,346% | Budget £420K |
| Emily Patterson | 60 min → 26 min per letter | 57 letters/year | Retires Dec 2026 |
| James Stewart | 800 completions/year | £1,575 fixed fee | Margin 18%→31% |
| Jordan Hayes | Error rate 6.5%→4.2%→<2% |
| David Harrison | PI £8,500 (2023) → £12,000 (2026) |

**4. Kill List & Voice**
- Zero Kill List violations
- Author voice preserved (lead author signs off)
- Flesch Reading Ease ≥ 75 (Ch09: ≥ 65)
